Delta Conveyance Science Plan COA 10.26

Incorporation of Fish Guidance System into the North Delta Intake Structures

COA 10.26 Revising Planning

Focal Species

Winter-run Chinook salmon Spring-run Chinook salmon Striped bass

Listed winter-run and spring-run Chinook salmon out-migrants are the target species a fish-guidance structure would be designed to protect; predator species (striped bass) are relevant because guidance structures can concentrate predation. The species framing is contested — CDFW objects that the sketch presupposes a guidance technology before the need is established.

Project Effect Links

Near-field. The north Delta intakes alter approach velocity, sweeping velocity, and the hydrodynamic field at the screen face. A floating fish-guidance structure (FFGS) is one candidate measure to reduce near-field impingement and improve guidance past the intakes — but whether such a structure is warranted is the open question this evaluation is meant to answer, not assume.

Objectives, Research Questions & Hypotheses

Convene a fish-guidance working group and evaluate whether, and how, a guidance system should be incorporated into the north Delta intake structures, providing CDFW a recommended approach no less than one year prior to finalizing 30% design. The evaluation moves from baseline velocity mapping through fish tracking, individual-based-model calibration, and behavioral-response evaluation to an FFGS design evaluation.

  1. Does near-field hydrodynamics and observed fish behavior at the intake screens indicate a need for an engineered fish-guidance measure?
  2. If a measure is warranted, which guidance configuration best improves guidance and survival without concentrating predation?
  3. Can the STAIRS individual-based model reliably predict fish response to candidate guidance configurations?

Background

The fish-guidance working group convenes at least two years prior to 30% design. The evaluation draws on the intake hydraulic models (COAs 10.25.1–10.25.4) and prior FFGS applications elsewhere in the Delta and Central Valley. The STAIRS individual-based model is proposed as the predictive tool, but it has not yet been independently reviewed.

Methods

Study area. The north Delta intake reach of the Sacramento River, focused on the near-field of the intake screen structures.

FFGS Evaluation

Methods / protocols
  • Baseline Velocity Mapping
  • Fish Tracking at the Intakes
  • STAIRS Individual-Based-Model Calibration
  • Behavioral-Response Evaluation
  • FFGS Design Evaluation
Performance metrics
  • Near-field approach and sweeping velocity distribution
  • Fish guidance efficiency by configuration
  • Predicted predation exposure by configuration
  • STAIRS calibration/validation goodness-of-fit

Data Management

Velocity-mapping, fish-tracking, and model-calibration datasets are versioned with provenance to the contributing intake hydraulic models; STAIRS model versions, calibration data, and validation results are archived for independent review.

Reporting Requirements

Draft Study Plan and Final Study Plan; a CDFW-approved recommended approach delivered no less than one year prior to finalizing 30% design. Working-group concurrence is required before advancing to design evaluation.
