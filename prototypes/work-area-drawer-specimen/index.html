This page now leads with the v2 deep dive — dates promoted to an always-visible band, a confirm that reads in two seconds, discipline cells that open a stacked child drawer, and a per-discipline review history that survives the June → September re-survey reality. Decision-first — v2 deep dive

  • Dates up high. Notification · Clearance visit · Planned start (+ Blocked until when relevant) render as a compact band directly under the header — decision inputs, not detail.
  • A two-second confirm. The provisional banner is now one slim line — observation ID · "within buffer · est. through Jul 24" · a compact Confirm block inline.
  • Disciplines open a stacked child drawer. Each board cell is a button that opens that discipline's own drawer over the parent (the permit workspace's segment → editor stacking) — not tabs, not one long form.
  • Review history per discipline. The child drawer holds every review — clearance survey, re-survey, management determination — newest first. The latest completed review sets the board status; future reviews ride the history as scheduled. Cells show a count when there is more than one. Survey records live here now, not in a parent section.

Data-model note (for productionizing)

Today's ClearanceGate holds one status per discipline — it cannot say "Blocked now, re-survey scheduled Sep 2." Productionizing v2 turns each discipline's gate into a per-discipline review list (date, kind — Clearance survey / Re-survey / Management determination — reviewer, outcome, notes/stipulations): the latest completed review derives the discipline's current status, and future reviews ride the history as scheduled. The fixture keeps its single-gate shape for now; the June → September story above is mocked statically in this specimen.

How to evaluate v2

  1. From a cold open, how fast do you know whether work can start — and what the single next action is?
  2. Are the dates you decide with — notification, visit, planned start, blocked-until — visible without opening anything?
  3. Does the stacked discipline drawer keep you oriented to the parent site?
  4. Is a June → September re-survey history legible at a glance?
  5. Where does a pasted field-biologist email note go, and could you find it again a week later?

Round 1 exploration (reference)

The three structural options as first presented — Decision-first (option 1) was selected and is developed at the top of the page. Kept for the record of what each structure prioritized.

1Decision-first

The top half answers one question: can work start — and if not, what single action moves it?

Prioritizes

  • The manager's go/no-go decision, with the ONE next action promoted to a real button
  • A four-cell discipline board you can scan in a second

Trades away

  • Ambient detail — conflicts, surveys, notes, dates and activity collapse until asked for

2Discipline workspace

One discipline at a time, at full depth — the drawer is a segmented workspace, not a stack.

Prioritizes

  • Per-discipline depth: status, dates, reviewer, notes and survey record in one panel
  • Clean per-discipline editing — add/edit/remove scoped to the open panel, no long form
  • A thin rollup strip keeps the site-level answer and the live conflict always in view

Trades away

  • Whole-site glanceability — you visit four panels to read everything

3Lifecycle / checklist

The work area is a pipeline — Notification → Survey → Determinations → Outcome → Work start — and every fact, conflict and edit lives at its step.

Prioritizes

  • Temporal clarity: where are we, what's next, what's late — dates on every step
  • Conflicts surface where they belong: the buffer conflict at Determinations, the start-before-review clash at Work start
  • Inline edits at the step — no separate edit mode

Trades away

  • Dense at-a-glance status across disciplines; the pipeline runs tall
