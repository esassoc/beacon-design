Executive Summary

Environmental Science Associates (ESA) is pleased to present Acme Energy with this Critical Issues Assessment (CIA) for the proposed Mojave Stockyard Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) Project. The Project would occupy a single 18.2-acre site (APN 237-104-007, 237-104-008) within the City of Mojave Stockyard, Kern County, California.

The purpose of this CIA is to identify environmental resources, federal, State, and local planning decisions, and potential development constraints that may impact Project feasibility, cost, or schedule. The CIA includes a desktop-level environmental characteristics discussion utilizing publicly available data from federal, State, and local sources. The list of resources considered is based on ESA's extensive experience with battery storage permitting in California and focuses on the resources and issues most likely to have substantial impact on feasibility, cost, or schedule. No field work was performed as part of this effort.

Following the environmental characteristics discussion in Section 3, critical issues and recommendations are summarized in Section 4. A summary of the permits and approvals that may be necessary for construction of the Project is presented in Section 5, and limitations to the CIA are presented in Section 6.

Summary of findings

    1. Project Description

    The Project site is located at 14250 Stockyard Road in the City of Mojave Stockyard, approximately 0.6 mile southeast of the existing Mojave Stockyard 230 kV substation. The site consists of two parcels (APN 237-104-007 and 237-104-008) totaling 18.2 acres of vacant land currently designated M-2 (Heavy Industrial). The site is bounded on the north and east by existing industrial uses, with a Class I rail spur to the south.

    The proposed Project would include approximately 1,000 battery enclosures arranged in fire-rated rows, inverter and transformer pads, a switchyard, a 36,000-gallon fire water tank, perimeter security fencing (8-foot chain link), and an access drive off Stockyard Road. No on-site staffing is planned during operations; the facility will be remotely operated with periodic site visits. The Project would interconnect to the existing 230 kV substation via a short underground gen-tie. The Project is currently in CAISO Interconnection Cluster 16. The Project site and study area are presented in Figure 1.

    The following sections summarize findings for each resource area considered in this CIA.     3.1 Land Use, Zoning & Planning

    Severity Medium Lead agency City of Mojave Stockyard, Planning Division

    Findings

    The Project site is designated M-2 (Heavy Industrial) under the City's zoning code, with an underlying General Plan land use designation of Industrial Employment Center. Battery energy storage is treated as a utility/industrial use and is permitted subject to a Conditional Use Permit (CUP) pursuant to recently adopted SB 283 implementing standards. The Project is consistent with applicable SCAG RTP/SCS goals relating to clean energy infrastructure and grid resilience. Setback and access requirements will need to be confirmed during the application phase, particularly regarding NFPA 855 separation distances and County Fire access standards.

    Recommendations

    ESA recommends Acme Energy request consultation and conceptual review with the City's Planning Division to confirm the CUP path, SB 283 timing requirements, and any anticipated conditions of approval. No General Plan Amendment or rezoning is anticipated.

    3.2 Aesthetics & Visual Resources

    Severity Low

    Findings

    No State scenic highways or locally designated scenic corridors traverse the area. The site sits within an existing industrial corridor with adjacent industrial development providing visual context. Public viewers are primarily limited to motorists along Stockyard Road. The City of Mojave Stockyard does not have a dark-sky overlay applicable to the Project area.

    Recommendations

    Standard design considerations for fencing, lighting (downcast / shielded), and structure reflectivity should be sufficient to address aesthetics. No formal visual resources technical report is anticipated to be required.

    3.3 Agricultural Resources

    Severity Low

    Findings

    The Project site is classified as Other Land under the California Department of Conservation Farmland Mapping and Monitoring Program (FMMP). There are no Williamson Act contracts associated with the parcels and no FMMP-designated Prime Farmland, Unique Farmland, or Farmland of Statewide Importance within or adjacent to the Project boundary.

    Recommendations

    No additional action is anticipated for agricultural resources.

    3.4 Biological & Aquatic Resources

    Severity High

    Findings

    The Project site lies adjacent to designated Critical Habitat for the Mohave ground squirrel (Federally listed) and within CNDDB sensitivity polygons for burrowing owl and western Joshua tree. A 5-mile CNDDB query also returned occurrences of desert tortoise, Le Conte's thrasher, Swainson's hawk (foraging only), and one record of prairie falcon. The site does not fall within a USFWS Habitat Conservation Plan (HCP) or CDFW Natural Community Conservation Planning (NCCP) program area.

    An ephemeral wash traverses the southwestern portion of the parcel as identified in the National Hydrography Dataset (NHD). The USFWS National Wetlands Inventory (NWI) identifies riverine features consistent with the NHD mapping. Following the Supreme Court decision in Sackett v. EPA, ephemeral washes such as those on the parcel are unlikely to be Waters of the U.S. and would not be subject to Section 404 permitting; however, the wash may be subject to State jurisdiction.

    Assistant note — the CNDDB Q2 2026 refresh added one new occurrence of western Joshua tree within the parcel that was not present in the prior release. Verify before finalizing.

    Recommendations

    ESA recommends a general biological reconnaissance survey to determine the presence or absence of habitat suitable for special status species on the Project site and within the direct proximity. The reconnaissance survey will inform whether focused protocol-level surveys are required, including Mohave ground squirrel, burrowing owl (CDFW 2012 protocol), and a western Joshua tree inventory. A biological technical report with mitigation measures is anticipated. If habitat areas suitable for desert tortoise are present and would be affected, a federal Incidental Take authorization may be required from USFWS under Section 7 or Section 10 of the Endangered Species Act. A Section 2081 Incidental Take Permit and/or a Western Joshua Tree Conservation Act (WJTCA) Incidental Take Permit may also be required from CDFW.

    ESA also recommends conducting an aquatic resources delineation. If the ephemeral wash cannot be avoided, a Section 1602 Streambed Alteration Agreement with CDFW and a 401 Water Quality Certification from the Lahontan RWQCB are likely to be required.

    3.5 Cultural & Tribal Resources

    Severity Medium

    Findings

    No known recorded cultural resources occur within the Project boundary based on publicly available data. One cultural sensitivity area is recorded within 1 mile. A records search request has been initiated with the South Central Coastal Information Center (SCCIC) of the California Historical Resources Information System (CHRIS); results are pending.

    Recommendations

    ESA recommends Acme Energy await SCCIC return before finalizing site disturbance plans. A pedestrian survey may be required depending on CHRIS results and City direction. AB 52 consultation should be initiated through the City as Lead Agency at the Notice of Preparation stage; consultation may last through the CEQA process. Tribal monitor coordination during ground disturbance should be anticipated.

    3.6 Hazardous Materials

    Severity High

    Findings

    The Project site is not listed in the DTSC EnviroStor or SWRCB GeoTracker databases. Within a 1-mile search radius, however, two closed Leaking Underground Storage Tank (LUST) sites and one open Voluntary Cleanup Program (VCP) case were identified.

    Recommendations

    The presence of hazardous materials should be verified with a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ASTM E1527-21) prior to grading-permit application. Review of DTSC EnviroStor and GeoTracker case files for closure conditions of nearby sites is recommended.

    3.7 Hydrology & Water Quality

    Severity Medium

    Findings

    The Project site is located within the Mojave Adjudicated groundwater basin. The NHD identifies one ephemeral wash crossing the southwestern portion of the parcel. The site is mapped as FEMA Zone X (outside the Special Flood Hazard Area). No Clean Water Act Section 303(d)-listed surface water segments would be affected.

    Recommendations

    A Drainage Study and Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) will be required to protect identified drainage systems and ensure runoff meets NPDES standards. Acme Energy should discuss the potential need for a Water Quality Management Plan with the City's Planning Division. Coverage under the SWRCB Construction General Permit (Order 2009-0009-DWQ) will be required prior to soil disturbance.

    3.8 Public Services & Wildfire

    Severity Medium

    Findings

    Fire protection services are provided by Kern County Fire. Based on CAL FIRE FHSZ data, the northeast corner of the Project boundary overlaps a Moderate FHSZ (SRA portion). The remainder of the site is outside designated FHSZ. The site is approximately 0.6 mile from the nearest existing substation. The County Fire department has reviewed two prior BESS projects in the jurisdiction.

    Recommendations

    Due to the nature of battery storage facilities, Kern County Fire will review project plans and provide comments during the permitting and approval process. Early consultation with County Fire regarding NFPA 855 compliance, emergency response planning, and water supply is recommended. SB 283 requires fire authority outreach at least 10 months prior to application submittal. Defensible-space setbacks and ignition-resistant construction should be documented on the site plan.
